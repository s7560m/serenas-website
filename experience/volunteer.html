VOLUNTEER

COMMUNITY SERVICE

My views on the world and my own self have developed through my six-year involvement with St. John Ambulance (SJA). SJA is an internationally recognized organization in which teaches and provides first aid and emergency medical services. Specifically the cadet program within SJA focuses of youths development in combining first aid and leadership training. The core of the organization is a set of disciplines, intertwined with serving the greater good that brings about positive change. In my six years with SJA, I have been involved in a many field ranging from internal to external affairs within the division. From providing first aid service during duties, event management, and becoming a classroom and drill instructor, these skills have taught me persistency and leadership, which have increased my confidence and reliability. I have taken part and placing first in numerous competitions throughout the years that have expanded my relationships with many people. I was apart of the Vancouver Flag Party for two years as the flag bearer to represent SJA for the City of Vancouver, during events such as the Remembrance Day Ceremony at Victory Square. I was also involved for two years in the administration team, where I expanded my understanding of Excel Programs and computer programing skills. From then on, I was responsible for allocating guest speakers to conduct presentations and I responsible for organizing Duke of Edinburgh trips, where I have been highly involved, participating in all bronze, silver, and gold trips. In recognition for my efforts, I was nominated from my division to apply for the most prestigious award given to one member in the whole lower mainland as the member representative. These experiences, in turn, have not only have allowed me to become more reflective, but I have also gained the value of cooperation in respecting differing opinions, and to bring about optimism, confidence, and trust in encouraging a team to push for success. In my view, leadership is about positively inspiring cooperation, and I hope to use these as a springboard to further develop my abilities to handle larger projects and expand my networking with others in professional settings.
